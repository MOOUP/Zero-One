一：开始投入资源/资金之前，需要弄清楚的问题：
   • Identify a specific social problem that you want to focus on 
     确定您想要关注的特定社会问题
   • Gather knowledge & research about the issue and consider its cause/s 
     收集有关该问题的知识和研究并考虑其原因
   • Be able to answer the questions:
     能够回答问题：
     1:Who is suffering? 
     2:What are they suffering from? 
     3:When did the suffering start? 
     4:Where are they located? 
     5:Why are they suffering? 
     6:How many people are affected?
     /
     1:谁在受苦?
     2:他们患有什么?
     3:苦难什什么时候开始?
     4:他们在哪里?
     5:他们为什么受苦?
     6:有多少⼈人受到影响?
    
   #Key Takeaways#
   • Before starting your venture, you should be able to accurately describe the problem and its cause(s)
   #关键要点#
   • 在开始创业之前，您应该能够准确地描述问题及其原因。

二：How do we come up with new ideas to solve the social problem we have identified?
    我们如何提出新想法来解决我们已经确定的社会问题?
  步骤一：
    1:Many problems have already been solved somewhere else in the world
      世界上其他地⽅方已经解决了了许多问题
    2:Thorough research of any/all past attempts
      彻底研究过去的所有尝试
      2.1, Opportunity to build off/modify earlier attempts
           建立/修改早期尝试的机会
      2.2, You owe it to you beneficiaries and supporters
           你欠你的受益者和支持者
    3:The closer your problem to the earlier solution, the more you can piggyback off existing, proven experience
      您的问题越接近早期的解决方案，您就越能够利用现有的、经过验证的经验
    4:There is no need to invent new solutions – the need is to generate funds by helping people
      没有必要发明新的解决方案 - 需要通过帮助人们来筹集资金
    5:Create your own idea only if you cannot find any examples of how other people have tried
      只有当你找不到别人如何尝试的例子时，你才可以创造自己的想法
   步骤二：Developing a Solution
          开发解决方案
    1:Going it alone, or going at it differently may be sheer ego trip
      单独行动,或以不同的方式可能是纯粹的自我旅行
    2:Usually dozens of programs have been tried out there
      通常有几十个程序在那里尝试过
    3:These are widely reported – for example see https://www.ashoka.org
      这些被广泛报道 - 例如参见https：//www.ashoka.org
    4:Look at successes – and see where you may copy parts of what has worked already, what problems they had to overcome BUT don’t blindly copy
      看成功,看看你可以复制的部分工作,有什么问题他们必须克服,但不要盲目地复制
    5:If you can, look at examples of obstacles and disappointments
      如果可以的话，看看障碍和失望的例子
      5.1, What did they do wrong
           他们做错了什么
      5.2, How might you avoid their mistakes?
           你怎么能避免他们的错误？
  步骤三：What if there are no examples we can build off?
         如果没有我们可以建立的例子怎么办？
    1:Revisit root cause questions you asked before: what, how, why, who, when, where
      重新审视您之前询问的根本原因问题：什么，如何，为什么，谁，何时，何地
    2:Now add some not questions:
      现在添加一些不是的问题：
      2.1, Who is not affected and why?
           谁不受影响，为什么？
      2.2, When does it not occur and why?
           什么时候不发生，为什么？
      2.3, Where does it not occur and why? 
           哪里没有出现？为什么？
    3:Time invested increases the richness and depth of understanding
      投入的时间增加了理解的丰富性和深度
    4:What might be changed so that this problem is reduced?
      有什么可以改变，以减少这个问题？
      4.1, What changed conditions will increase the chances problem will not occur?
           什么条件的改变会增加问题不会发生的机会?
  步骤四：Map Actions
         制定行动
    A1:Map out actions you can take that will reduce the incidence of the problem – do as many below as you are able
      制定可以采取的措施，以减少问题的发生率 - 尽可能多地执行以下操作
    A2:How can you:
      你能做的：
      2.1, Remove reasons why people are affected?
           消除人们受影响的原因?
      2.2, Ameliorate or lessen how people are affected?
           改善或减少人们受影响的程度?
      2.3, Reduce or improve on who will be affected?
           减少或改善谁将受到影响?
      2.4, Reduce the times when people will be affected?
           减少人们受影响的时间?
      2.5, Reduce the places where people will be affected?
           减少人们受影响的地方?
    A3:In each case, also ask how your solutions to the questions could be used to generate revenue or funding
       在每种情况下，也要问一问你的问题的解决方案如何能被用来产生收入或资金
    B:Map all answers to your “what to change” questions on a summarizing chart:
      将你“应该改变什么”问题的所有答案映射到一个总结图表上:
    B1:List all the possible changes that will ameliorate the problem
       列出可以改善问题的所有可能的更改
    B2:Think about how you might generate revenues or funding from these changes. Who may be willing and able to provide revenues?
       考虑一下如何从这些变化中获得收入或资金。谁可能愿意并能够提供收入?
    B3:List all the sources of revenues
       列出所有收入来源
    C1:Now select the three to five most impactful changes that you can implement
       现在选择您可以实施的三到五个最具影响力的更改
    C2:Put together a one page statement that describes what changes you want to implement to attack the social problem and to generate funds in doing so
       汇总一页的声明，描述您要实施哪些变更来攻击社会问题并为此产生资金
    C3:This is your first-cut proposed solution!
       这是您提出的第一个解决方案！
    *You may realize your idea will not work and you may need to start again*
    *您可能会意识到您的想法不起作用，您可能需要重新开始*
    
   #Key Takeaways#
   1:No need to invent – Try to build off others’ ideas but tailor them to your specific situation
     无需发明 - 尝试建立其他人的想法，但根据您的具体情况量身定制
   2:Do thorough search for similar projects that have been tried in the past
     彻底搜索过去尝试过的类似项目
   3:Learn what led to success and/or disappointment for these others
     了解是什么导致了其他人的成功和/或失望
   4:Decided what you need to and can change and how to fund this change
     决定你需要改变什么，可以改变什么，以及如何资助这种改变
     
三：What if our solution is wrong?
   如果我们的解决⽅方案错了了怎么办
   步骤一：
     A:Closing Admonition
     A:关闭警告
       1, Make no mistake – building a social enterprise is a LOT of work
          毫无疑问——构建一个社会企业是很多工作
       2，Be prepared to do many starts and stops
          准备做很多启动和停止
       3，Be prepared to do detailed work that has to be redone as you progress
          准备做详细的工作必须重做你的进步
       4，Be prepared to experience and overcome disappointments along the way
          准备经验,克服失望
       5，If you are not willing to do the detailed work, and to revisit it as you progress, all you are likely to do is end up as a failure that has parasitically used scarce resources others could have used better
          如果你不愿意做详细的工作，并且随着你的进步而重新审视它，你可能会以失败告终，因为你寄生地使用了稀缺的资源，而其他人本可以使用得更好
     B:Start Assembling an Advisory Group
     B:开始组建咨询小组
       1:Think of people you must turn to for help, advice, and feedback:
         想想你必须寻求帮助、建议和反馈的人:
         1.1, Early on recruit “on the ground” advisors = who understand the environment: market, culture, local legal issues, politics
              早期招募“实地”顾问=了解环境:市场、文化、当地法律问题、政治
         1.2, Functional advisors to help you with technical and business issues (accounting, law, financing) among others
              功能顾问，帮助您解决技术和业务问题（会计，法律，融资）等问题
       2:Start out with maybe only a few informal advisors, and along the way you can add more
         从一些非正式的顾问开始，你可以添加更多
